# Overview Dashboard

The Overview is your YouTube home base — a quick look at the numbers that matter most over the last 30 days.

---

## What You'll See

### The Three Stat Cards

At the top, three cards give you your headline numbers:

**Total Views**
How many times your videos were watched in the last 30 days, combined across your whole channel. The small sparkline graph underneath shows whether you're trending up or down over time.

**Watch Hours**
The total number of hours people spent watching your content. This matters more than raw views — YouTube's algorithm rewards watch time heavily. A video that keeps people watching for 10 minutes is worth far more than one they click off after 30 seconds.

**Net Subscribers**
New subscribers minus people who unsubscribed — your real growth number for the period. A positive number means your channel is growing. A negative one is worth paying attention to.

---

### Daily Views Chart

Below the stat cards is a chart showing your view count day by day for the last 30 days. This is great for spotting:

- A sudden spike (a video went viral, or got recommended by YouTube)
- A consistent upward or downward trend
- Days where you got significantly more or less traffic than usual

---

### Recent Videos

The bottom section shows your 5 most recent uploads with their thumbnails and current view counts. Click any video to jump straight to its retention analysis in Retention Studio.

---

## Changing the Date Range

By default everything shows the last 30 days. You can adjust this with the date selector in the header to see 7 days, 90 days, or a custom range.

---

## "No Data Yet" State

If you just connected and the dashboard shows empty charts, just wait a minute — the first sync is still running. Hit the **Sync** button to speed it up.

If your channel is very new or has very few views, some metrics may show as zero or flat. That's normal — the data will fill in as your channel grows.
