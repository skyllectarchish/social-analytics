# Archive Miner

Archive Miner scans your older videos against today's trending topics and tells you which ones are sitting on a goldmine — because a topic you covered years ago is suddenly trending again.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Every creator has a graveyard of old videos that performed okay when they posted them, then faded into obscurity. But here's the thing: topics go in and out of fashion. Something you covered in 2022 might be exactly what people are searching for right now.

Manually checking every old video against current trends? Impossible. Archive Miner does it automatically, every week.

---

## What You'll See

Go to **Archive Miner** in the sidebar. You'll see a grid of suggestion cards — one for each old video that has a detected revival opportunity.

### A Suggestion Card Looks Like This:

**Video title**
The original title of your old video.

**Action badge (color-coded)**
One of three recommendations:
- 🟣 **Remake** — the topic is surging enough that a fresh, updated video would likely outperform the original. Make it a proper new upload.
- 🔴 **Clip to Short** — a specific moment from the video could work as a YouTube Short targeting the trending keyword. Quick win, no filming needed.
- 🟡 **Update** — you don't need to remake the video. Just update the title, description, or chapters to capture the new search traffic. 15-minute job, no production required.

**Topic tag**
The trending keyword that triggered the suggestion, like `#mechanical keyboards` or `#AI tools`.

**Wikipedia spike badge** (green)
If shown, it means the Wikipedia page for this topic saw a big jump in traffic recently (e.g., "+45% Wikipedia"). This signals broad internet interest — news coverage, mainstream media, something drove people to look it up.

**YouTube search badge** (blue)
If shown, it means the topic is actively appearing in YouTube's autocomplete suggestions right now, like `YT search: "best mechanical keyboards 2026"`. This means people are actively searching for it on YouTube.

**AI Recommendation**
A 1-2 sentence specific action from the AI, like:
> *"Your 2022 mechanical keyboard review covers this topic perfectly. Consider remaking it as 'Best Mechanical Keyboards for Beginners 2026' — the +45% Wikipedia spike suggests mainstream interest, and the long-tail queries in autocomplete show buyers are looking for entry-level options."*

## How It Works Under the Hood (Without the Jargon)

Every week, Archive Miner runs through your videos older than 1 year. For each one, it:

1. **Figures out the topic.** The AI reads the title and description and extracts the core keyword — like "mechanical keyboards" from "My 10 Favorite Mechanical Keyboards of 2023."

2. **Checks YouTube search demand.** It queries YouTube's autocomplete to see if that topic is actively being searched for right now. If "mechanical keyboards" shows up in YouTube's suggestions with lots of variations, demand is healthy.

3. **Checks global internet interest.** It looks up the Wikipedia page for the topic and checks whether pageviews have spiked in the last 7 days vs the previous 3 weeks. A spike means something drove mainstream attention to this topic — a product launch, a viral moment, news coverage.

4. **Only flags real opportunities.** If neither signal fires (no autocomplete activity, no Wikipedia spike), the video is skipped. You only see suggestions for topics that are *actually trending right now*, not just "still somewhat searchable."

5. **Writes an actionable recommendation.** If both signals fire, the AI figures out whether to suggest a Remake, a Short, or just an Update — based on how strong the trend is.

> **Why Wikipedia?** Wikipedia pageviews are a surprisingly reliable, completely free signal for "is the internet buzzing about this topic right now?" When "Mechanical keyboard" articles see a 50% spike in views, it usually means major tech news or a viral moment drove a wave of mainstream interest — which directly correlates with YouTube search intent.

## Running a Manual Scan

The Archive Miner runs automatically every Sunday night. But if you want to check right now — maybe you just saw a trend breaking in your niche — click the **Run Scan Now** button at the top of the page.

The scan takes a minute or two. When it's done, the suggestions grid will refresh with new results.

> **Note:** You can only run a manual scan once per day. If you already ran one today, the button will be grayed out until tomorrow.

---

## The "Last Scanned" Timestamp

You'll see a small timestamp near the top of the page showing when the last scan ran — for example, "Last scanned Jun 4." This tells you how fresh your suggestions are.

If it says "Never scanned," just hit Run Scan Now or wait for the automatic Sunday run.

## What to Do With a Remake Suggestion

When Archive Miner suggests a Remake:

1. **Don't just re-upload the old video** — update it. New information, current examples, 2026 context. Think of it as a spiritual sequel.
2. **Use the trending keyword in your title.** The AI usually suggests a specific title — use it as a starting point, then make it yours.
3. **Add a card or end screen in the old video** pointing to the new one. You'll capture any traffic the old video still gets.

---

## What to Do With a Short Suggestion

When Archive Miner suggests a Clip to Short:

1. Identify the most punchy, self-contained moment in the video — ideally something that works without context.
2. Trim it to under 60 seconds, add captions, and post it as a Short.
3. Link back to the original in the description.

---

## What to Do With an Update Suggestion

When Archive Miner suggests an Update:

1. Open the video in YouTube Studio.
2. Update the **title** to include the trending keyword. Don't make it feel forced — just work it in naturally.
3. Update the **description** with the trending terms in the first 2-3 lines (that's what YouTube indexes most heavily).
4. If the video has chapters, update the chapter titles to include relevant keywords.

This can boost a stale video to the top of search results with zero production work.
