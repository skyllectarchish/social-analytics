# Retention Studio

Retention Studio goes beyond showing you *where* viewers drop off — it tells you *why*, using AI to analyze your video transcript at every significant drop-off point.

---

## What Is Audience Retention?

Audience retention is the percentage of your viewers still watching at any moment in your video. If your retention curve drops sharply at 2:15, it means a bunch of people left the video right at that moment.

YouTube Studio shows you this curve too — but it stops there. It doesn't tell you *what happened at 2:15* that made people leave. Retention Studio does.

---

## How to Use Retention Studio

1. Go to **Retention Studio** in the sidebar.
2. On the left, you'll see a list of your videos. Search or scroll to find the one you want to analyze.
3. Click a video. The retention chart loads on the right.
4. Scroll below the chart to see the AI explanations for each drop-off.

---

## Reading the Chart

**The red curve** — your actual retention. Starts at 100% and gradually falls as viewers stop watching. A curve that stays high for a long time = great content. Sharp drops = something went wrong at that moment.

**The dashed gray line** — the YouTube benchmark. This shows how an average video of similar length performs on YouTube. If your curve is above the dashed line, you're doing better than the competition. Below it? There's room to improve.

**The vertical lines** — these mark the drop-off points where InfluenceIQ detected a significant viewer loss. Each one has an AI explanation below the chart.

---

## The AI Drop-Off Explanations

This is the part YouTube Studio doesn't have. Under the chart, you'll see cards like:

> **4:32 — 12% of viewers left here**
> "The pacing slowed significantly during the sponsor read. Viewers who stayed through the intro left when the energy dropped and the topic changed abruptly. Consider shortening this segment or leading with the sponsor's most interesting hook."

Or:

> **0:48 — 18% of viewers left here**
> "Your hook asked a question but didn't hint at the answer quickly enough. Viewers lost confidence the video would deliver what the title promised. Try teasing the payoff within the first 30 seconds."

The AI reads the exact words being spoken at that timestamp and uses them to explain the drop. It's not guessing — it's reading your transcript.

---

## Why Some Videos Don't Show Retention Data

Retention analysis requires **at least 1,000 views** on the video. Below that threshold, YouTube's data isn't statistically reliable — the sample size is too small to draw meaningful conclusions, and the AI explanations would be misleading.

If you see "No retention data available for this video," it just means the video needs more views before the analysis becomes useful.

---

## What "Analyzing with AI..." Means

When you see a spinner that says "Analyzing with AI — check back in a moment," the AI is currently processing the retention curve for that video. This usually takes 30–60 seconds the first time. Refresh the page after a minute and the explanations will be there.

---

## How to Use This Information

The retention analysis is most useful when you look for patterns across multiple videos:

- **If the same timestamp structure always causes drops** (e.g., the 3-4 minute mark on all your long-form videos), you may have a pacing habit to break.
- **If your first 30 seconds have the highest drop-off**, your hooks aren't landing — people aren't convinced early on that the video is worth their time.
- **If retention spikes (goes above 1.0)** at certain moments, people are rewinding to rewatch. That's a signal of a particularly engaging or confusing moment — worth studying.
- **If you consistently beat the benchmark**, you're already doing something right. Study those videos to find the formula.

---

## The Benchmark Line — What Does It Mean?

The gray dashed line compares your video to YouTube videos of similar length in the same general category. Being *above* the benchmark means your content retains viewers better than average for videos like yours. Being below it doesn't mean you failed — it means there's a specific gap to close, and the drop-off explanations point you toward it.
